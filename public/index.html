Rising Tides

A web-based application to showcase rising water levels in Boston neighborhoods by integrating situated visualization, augmented reality, and public participation technology. We focus on creating an accessible platform for localized climate change and visual discourse, especially for underserved communities in the East Boston area.

Overview

Situated visualization (SV) brings visualization in situ, connecting people and spaces. Augmented reality (AR) is an interactive experience combining real-world environments with computer-generated content. Combined with AR, situated visualization can transcend linear timelines and present predicted future information. We will explore novel approaches to bridge SV, AR, and civic technology to raise awareness and enable open discourse around the impact of climate change on Boston. Our goal is to design, develop, and deploy a mobile platform that simulates the localized impact of climate change on Boston residents in the future to (1) enable the residents to experience visualizations of climate change effects and (2) empower them to share comments and ideas on climate change issues. The platform will benefit the movement towards a more equitable resilience and will create new opportunities for the public, especially the underserved communities, to raise their voices and join the discourse.

Who We Are

Professor Narges Mahyar

Narges Mahyar is an assistant professor at the Manning College of Information and Computer Sciences, University of Massachusetts Amherst. Currently, she is a Radcliffe fellow at Harvard University. Narges specializes in a multidisciplinary research area that spans human-computer interaction, information visualization, social computing, applied machine learning, human-centered artificial intelligence, and design. She applies a community-centered design approach to build novel social computing and visualization tools to empower the general public to engage in real-world sociotechnical issues, such as urban planning and climate change, by enabling them to share their ideas and comments for shaping future policies. During her Radcliffe fellowship, she is investigating innovative techniques to integrate situated visualization, augmented reality, and civic technology to design and build an accessible platform that simulates the localized impact of climate change, thereby providing Boston residents with an immersive experience of climate change visualizations and empowering them to contribute comments and ideas on climate change issues.
